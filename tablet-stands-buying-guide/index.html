How to choose a tablet stands

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

To choose a tablet stand, focus on adjustability (height, angle, rotation), stability (weighted base or sturdy build), and compatibility with your device size and case. Match the stand to your primary use case, desk work, bed/couch viewing, or travel, and don’t overpay for features you won’t use.

What should you look for in a tablet stand?

The three pillars are adjustability, stability, and compatibility. Adjustability means you can tilt the screen to reduce glare, raise it to eye level, and swivel for sharing. Stability keeps your device from tipping when you tap or type. Compatibility covers both the physical size of your tablet (with or without a case) and whether the stand’s grip or cradle accommodates it. Don’t overlook portability if you move between rooms or travel. A lightweight, foldable stand is great for coffee shops, but a heavier desk stand with a non-slip base stays put during long work sessions. Think about where you’ll use it most, the right trade‑off depends on that context.

Do you need a heavy‑duty stand or a lightweight portable stand?

Heavy‑duty stands, often with a steel base and wide arms, are ideal for a permanent desk setup. They resist wobble when you’re using a keyboard or stylus, and many include cable management or a built‑in charging pad. The downside is weight, you won’t want to pack one in a bag. Lightweight portable stands fold flat or collapse into a compact shape. They trade some stability for convenience and are perfect for travel, bedside use, or the kitchen counter. Look for a design that uses friction hinges or a solid locking mechanism so your tablet doesn’t sag during video calls or reading.

How does adjustability affect your comfort?

Ergonomics matter. A stand that lets you adjust height and viewing angle reduces neck and shoulder strain. For typing, you want the tablet’s screen near eye level and your hands at a natural angle. For drawing or note‑taking, a low, tiltable surface is better. Most good stands offer tilt ranges from nearly flat (about 10 degrees) to almost vertical (roughly 80 degrees). Some include a rotation feature so you can switch between portrait and landscape without moving the base. If you share your screen often, a stand with 360‑degree swivel saves hassle.

What materials and build quality matter?

Aluminum and zinc alloy stands feel premium and hold up to daily use. They’re also heavier, which helps stability. Plastic stands are lighter and more affordable, but may flex or degrade over time. The best plastic designs use reinforced joints and rubberized grips to compensate. Non‑slip pads on the base and cradle are essential, they keep both the stand and your tablet from sliding. Check that the arm or hinge is smooth and locks firmly. A stand that slowly droops under a heavy iPad is a common frustration.

Should you consider a stand with a charging feature?

Some stands integrate wireless charging pads, which can cut cable clutter if you have a compatible device. The charger is typically in the base, so your tablet sits above it. This works well for iPhones and Android phones, but many larger tablets don’t charge wirelessly, so the pad becomes a phone spot only. Cable management is another plus: a desktop stand with a routed channel keeps your charging cord tidy. Stands with built‑in batteries are rare and often expensive, a standard stand paired with a separate power bank is usually more flexible.

How do you match a stand to your specific device?

Measure your tablet with its case on. Many stands have a maximum width and depth; if your device is too thick or too wide, it won’t sit securely. For iPads Pro 12.9” or larger Android tablets, look for stands that explicitly support extra‑large devices, some are designed for phones only. If you use a keyboard case, the tablet’s weight distribution changes. A clamp‑style stand can hold uneven shapes better than a simple groove. Always check the stand’s grip style: adjustable spring‑loaded arms accommodate varied sizes, while fixed cradles are more stable but less flexible.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use a tablet stand with a case on?

Yes, but check the stand’s maximum width and thickness. Many stands work fine with slim cases, but rugged or keyboard cases may require a stand with adjustable arms or a wider cradle. Remove very thick cases to be safe.

Are tablet stands compatible with all tablets?

Not automatically. Some stands are designed only for phones or small tablets, while others hold devices up to 12.9 inches. Always check the manufacturer’s stated size range. Most popular stands from reputable brands cover the common iPad and Galaxy Tab sizes.

What is the ideal height for a tablet stand?

It depends on your seating height and desk surface. For a standard desk, a stand that lifts the tablet’s center to roughly eye level, around 12 to 18 inches from the desk surface, works well. Adjustable stands let you fine‑tune this.

Do I need a weighted base for stability?

If you plan to tap or type on the screen, yes. Weighted or wide bases prevent tipping. For reading or passive viewing, a lighter stand is fine. Heavy‑duty stands are overkill if you only use your tablet on a stable tabletop without pressing the screen.

Are there tablet stands for bed or floor use?

Yes. Floor stands with a long adjustable arm and a weighted base let you use your tablet hands‑free while lying down or sitting on the floor. Bed trays with a built‑in groove are another option, they’re less expensive but less adjustable.

Can a tablet stand also hold a phone?

Many tablet stands can hold a phone, especially those with spring‑loaded arms. Dedicated phone stands are usually smaller and cheaper. If you want a single stand for both, look for one that explicitly lists phone compatibility and has enough padding to avoid scratches.

In shortThe best tablet stand isn’t the most expensive or the flashiest, it’s the one that fits your device, your desk, and your daily habits. Start with adjustability and stability, then match the build to your environment. A well‑chosen stand will save your neck, declutter your workspace, and make your tablet feel like a real computer.
